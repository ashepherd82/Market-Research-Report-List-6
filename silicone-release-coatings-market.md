<p><p>Silicone release coatings are categorized based on their curing mechanisms. Solvent addition cure involves incorporating a solvent to facilitate curing, typically resulting in a flexible finish. Solventless addition cure eliminates solvents, offering a more environmentally friendly option with quick curing times. Emulsion addition cure uses emulsified silicone for easier application and reduced solvent emissions, appealing for high-performance needs. Other market types may include novel formulations or hybrid systems designed for specific applications, addressing diverse industry demands.</p></p>
