<p><p>The Two-Wheeler Anti-Braking System (ABS) market is categorized into two primary types: Front Wheel Anti-Braking System and Rear Wheel Anti-Braking System. The Front Wheel ABS focuses on preventing wheel lock-up during braking, enhancing stability and control, especially in emergency situations. In contrast, the Rear Wheel ABS primarily targets rear wheel instability, helping to maintain traction and reducing the risk of skidding. Both systems improve safety and rider confidence, driving their adoption in modern two-wheeler vehicles.</p></p>
