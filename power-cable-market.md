<p><p>A power cable is an electrical cable used to transmit and distribute electrical power. It consists of one or more conductors, typically made of copper or aluminum, insulated with materials like PVC, XLPE, or rubber, and often shielded for protection against environmental factors. Power cables are essential in various applications, including residential, commercial, industrial, and infrastructure projects, ensuring reliable electricity supply.</p><p>The Power Cable Market is experiencing significant growth, driven by increasing demand for electricity, urbanization, and infrastructure development worldwide. The market is expected to grow at a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 8.1% during the forecast period. Key factors contributing to this growth include the expansion of renewable energy projects, such as solar and wind farms, which require extensive power cable networks for energy transmission. Additionally, the modernization of aging power grids and the rise of smart grid technologies are fueling demand for advanced power cables.</p><p>Emerging trends in the market include the development of high-voltage direct current (HVDC) cables for long-distance power transmission and the adoption of eco-friendly, fire-resistant, and low-smoke zero-halogen (LSZH) cables to meet stringent safety and environmental regulations. The Asia-Pacific region is leading the market due to rapid industrialization and urbanization, while North America and Europe are focusing on upgrading their power infrastructure and integrating renewable energy sources.</p></p>
