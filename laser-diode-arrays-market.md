<p><p>The laser diode arrays market is segmented into microchannel cooled, conductively cooled, and fluid cooled types, each catering to different performance and application needs. Microchannel cooling utilizes small channels for efficient heat dissipation, enhancing thermal management in compact designs. Conductively cooled systems rely on solid materials to transfer heat away from the diodes, suitable for lower power applications. Fluid cooled systems use liquid to provide superior cooling, making them ideal for high-power lasers in industrial and scientific applications.</p></p>
