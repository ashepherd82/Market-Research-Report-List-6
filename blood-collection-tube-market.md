EDTA tubes are utilized for hematology and blood chemistry analyses, as EDTA acts as an anticoagulant. Plasma separation tubes are used to separate plasma from cellular components, facilitating diagnostic procedures. Additionally, "other" tubes encompass specialty tubes employed for specific tests, including those with additives for different analyses and preservation methods.</p></p>

<p><p>The blood collection tube market primarily serves two applications: venous and capillary blood collection. Venous blood collection involves drawing blood from large veins, commonly used for diagnostic testing and transfusions. It necessitates specialized tubes designed to preserve sample integrity. Capillary blood collection, on the other hand, involves obtaining blood from smaller capillaries, usually via fingerstick or heel prick, suitable for point-of-care testing and pediatric applications. Both methods require specific tube types to ensure accurate results and effective storage.</p></p>
