<p><p>Magneto Resistive RAM (MRAM) is a non-volatile memory technology that uses magnetic states to store data, offering high speed and low power consumption. The market is primarily divided into two types: Toggle MRAM and Spin-Transfer Torque MRAM (STT-MRAM). Toggle MRAM employs magnetic toggling to switch states, providing high endurance but slower speeds. In contrast, STT-MRAM utilizes spin currents to manipulate magnetic layers, resulting in faster data access and lower power usage, making it more suitable for advanced applications.</p></p>
