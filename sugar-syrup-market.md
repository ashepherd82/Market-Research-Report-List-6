<p><p>The sugar syrup market encompasses various types, each with distinct characteristics and uses. Glucose syrup, derived from hydrolyzed starch, serves as a sweetener and thickener in food production. Corn syrup, a high-fructose variant, enhances flavor and moisture in confections. Maple syrup, sourced from maple tree sap, offers a unique taste and is popular in breakfast foods. Cane syrup, extracted from sugar cane, is used for its rich flavor in desserts. Other syrups include agave and honey, catering to diverse consumer preferences.</p></p>
