<p><p>Cold Isostatic Pressing (CIP) equipment encompasses two main types: dry bag pressing and wet bag pressing. In dry bag pressing, materials are placed in a dry elastomeric bag, where pressure is applied to achieve uniform compaction. Wet bag pressing utilizes a liquid medium, typically water, to surround the material, enhancing the pressing process through hydrostatic pressure. Each method serves various applications in powder metallurgy, ceramics, and advanced materials, providing different benefits regarding density and mechanical properties.</p></p>
