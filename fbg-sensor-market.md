<p><p>FBG (Fiber Bragg Grating) sensors are advanced optical sensors that utilize the principles of fiber optics to measure physical parameters such as temperature, strain, and pressure. These sensors consist of a series of reflective grating patterns inscribed along an optical fiber, which reflect specific wavelengths of light. Changes in environmental conditions alter the wavelength of the reflected light, allowing for precise measurements.</p><p>The FBG sensor market is experiencing significant growth, driven by increasing applications in various sectors, including civil engineering, aerospace, and healthcare. <p><p>The fiber Bragg grating (FBG) sensor market is primarily categorized into point FBG sensors and distributed FBG sensors. Point FBG sensors are used for localized measurements, providing precise data on specific points such as temperature or strain. In contrast, distributed FBG sensors utilize a long optical fiber, allowing for continuous monitoring along the entire length of the fiber, making them ideal for applications like structural health monitoring. Both types offer unique advantages depending on the application requirements.</p></p>
