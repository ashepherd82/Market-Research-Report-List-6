<p><p>The Overt Hepatic Encephalopathy market comprises three primary types: Type A, Type B, and Type C. Type A pertains to acute liver failure, where rapid degradation in liver function leads to severe neurological symptoms. Type B involves portal systemic bypass without underlying liver disease, resulting in diminished hepatic filtration and potential encephalopathy. Type C is characterized by liver cirrhosis, where chronic liver damage leads to impaired detoxification, significantly increasing the risk of encephalopathic episodes. Each type necessitates unique therapeutic approaches.</p></p>
