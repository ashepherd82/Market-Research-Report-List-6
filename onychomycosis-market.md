<p><p>Onychomycosis is a fungal infection affecting nails, categorized into several types. Distal Subungual Onychomycosis, the most common, appears as thickened, discolored nails often due to dermatophytes. White Superficial Onychomycosis presents as chalky white patches on the nail surface. Proximal Subungual Onychomycosis affects the nail base, often seen in immunocompromised individuals. Candida Onychomycosis involves yeast infections, primarily impacting fingernails. The "Others" category encompasses less common forms and variations of fungal nail infections, reflecting the diverse nature of this condition.</p></p>
