<p><ul><li>CIP Method</li><li>Vibration Molding Method</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The isotropic graphite market features two primary manufacturing methods: the Cold Isostatic Pressing (CIP) method and Vibration Molding Method. The CIP method involves applying uniform pressure to graphite powder in a liquid medium, producing denser, more uniform materials ideal for high-performance applications. In contrast, the Vibration Molding Method uses vibration to compact graphite powder within a mold, enhancing flow and reducing porosity. Both methods cater to diverse applications, including electronics, aerospace, and energy sectors, emphasizing strength and thermal conductivity.</p></p>
