<p><p>The friction modifier market is categorized into two main types: organic and inorganic friction modifiers. Organic friction modifiers are derived from carbon-based compounds, often utilized in applications like lubricants and automotive fluids to enhance performance and reduce wear. In contrast, inorganic friction modifiers consist of materials such as sulfides or metal oxides, offering distinct benefits like improved thermal stability and resistance to high pressures. Both types play crucial roles in optimizing friction control and enhancing the longevity of machinery and automotive components.</p></p>
